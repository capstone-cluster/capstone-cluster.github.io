Week of May 4th

May 7, 2020 by Samuel Matylewicz

We had our last meeting with Dr. Cotton on Tuesday and he seems very excited with our progress. The cluster sucessfully deployed on the C7000 with a slurm master and VMs with ESXi on each blade. There is much more about this on Adam's blog, but it would be fun in the future to take his project and switch from ESXi to Proxmox and then run his same tests to try and achieve better results. To get Proxmox working properly we had to use the REST API to handle the VMs and keep them a layer away from Proxmox. This adds a layer of complexity to the system and constraining the system like this may take away from some of the Proxmox features that make it excel when compared to ESXi. A main takeaway from this semester and our project as a whole is that there are so many ways to configure and launch a cluster. Each way has pros and cons and are very system specific. Now that everything is working on the C7000, the roots are there, I hope that Dr. Cotton and future students play with the configuration and deployment options to test and record results and find a maximum efficeincy setup.

Week of April 20th

April 25, 2020 by Samuel Matylewicz

From what we have researched Proxmox is more efficient than ESXi, our team was interested in showing some results to back this up. Testing is no longer in the project scope because we do not have access to the 3 Dell towers (coronavirus) and the delay in receiving the C7000. People believe that Proxmox is taking over the market because it is open source, free to use, and more flexible. One of the biggest benefits is that Proxmox treats every node as a master node. This is why we tried to implement it in our project. Dan and I learned that although Proxmox has great reviews, there is significantly more literature (especially related to older hardware) for ESXi. Although implementation prooved rather difficult, we eventually got the script running to have Proxmox grab a master node and tell it to deploy a worker VM. While Adam and Theo continued with ESXi to complete the deliverables, Dan and I continued working on Proxmox to proove the theory and leave the project open for improvement. In the future, a team could implement Proxmox on the C7000 and R805 and run deployment testing to see if the clustering efficeincy has improved.
